Learning to Explain without Structured Knowledge


About L2E

Learning to provide plausible explanations is a challenging but critical goal for language based human-machine interaction because humans often demand explanations of the world. Explanations are challenging in that they require many different forms of abstract knowledge and reasoning. We contribute to the under-explored area of generating natural language explanations for general phenomena. We automatically collect large datasets of explanation-phenomenon pairs, which allow us to train modern end-to-end models to generate natural language explanations. We compare different training strategies and evaluate their performance using both automatic scores and human ratings.
